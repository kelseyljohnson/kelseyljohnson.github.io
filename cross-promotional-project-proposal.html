Cross Promotional Project Proposal

This is a project created, developed, proposed, and managed by Kelsey L. Johnson.

Vision

Use snippets/commercials read by hosts to cross-promote our podcasts. Easy for us to do, low time commitment, potentially big increase in listens for each show.

Examples

(Audio File Included)

  • NFL podcasts: 10 or 15 second blurb at the beginning or end of the podcast
  • Maximum Fun (a comedy podcast network I listen to): Do a commercial in place of another advertisement
  • Network TV: Promoting their own shows in commercial breaks
  • Gimlet Media: Playing actual segments of other shows (might not work for our audience)

Process

Have hosts write a 15 second or 90 word description/promotion of their show. We can also draft these and have the hosts sign off on them

Start slow, maybe with one or two shows or categories cross-promotion. We’re thinking [EXAMPLE HOST]'s' shows. Note: Head audio engineer doesn’t think it would be hard to jump straight in but starting slow could be a good proof of concept.

Hosts (sell it as a promotion of their podcast) record episode as usual. The guest hangs up. We quickly record cross-promo tease.
“Hey, this is John and this is Sharon. We’re the hosts of Digital Detectives, a show about ediscovery, information security, and data science as it relates to the law. If you’re interested in legal technology, find our show and more on legaltalknetwork.com, iTunes, or wherever you listen to podcasts.”
Each host only has to record this once and we can keep it to insert into other shows.

Resources/Time Spent Internally

Executive Producer:

  • Draft/send a small script to each host likely to participate along with a brief explanation of the program.
  • Field any questions and provide explanations about the program to hosts.
  • Potential backup recording of cross promotion as “our producer”.
  • Projected time: 1 hour setup per show and 5hrs/year ongoing.

Head Engineer:

  • Take snippet, have show music playing in the background for audio branding, can create the promos as a commercial and they will take less than 5 minutes to add into the episodes.
  • Projected time: 3 hours setup per show and 30hrs/year ongoing.

Marketing Manager (me):

  • Manage project and write/edit hosts scripts.
  • Set up spreadsheet to track when each cross promotion is done.
  • Track stats to show results of the promotion.
  • Projected time: 2 hours setup per show and 5hrs/year ongoing (general management and tracking time).

Categories

The idea here is to only cross promote on podcasts where the listenership might already be interested. Legal technology is the most obvious example of this.
